BUKIT SIPATIR

With more than 70 per cent of the land in Brunei Darussalam covered in pristine jungle. There are many different hiking trails in this country where one can go and escape from the world while exploring and experiencing the blissful nature of our nation.

Among the hiking trails around the Sultanate are Bukit Shahbandar Forest Recreational Park, Berakas; Tasek Lama Recreational Park in Bandar Seri Begawan; Wasai Wong Kadir Recreational Park, Labi; Bukit Batu Bujang Pahang; Bukit Patoi Forest Recreational Park; Bukit Saeh; and the latest addition to Brunei’s popular hiking tracks – Bukit Sipatir.

Bukit Sipatir has attracted more than 2,500 visitors during the peak period and is now a must-visit destination for the country.

The summit is used for relaxation and scenery, from where it can see Brunei River, the new Temburong bridge, Brunei Bay and Matan River and across the land, hikers get panoramic views of Kampong Subok. Interestingly, food and beverage stalls can be found near the car park and 'Sipatir' clothes are also on sale.

BUKIT SARANG HELANG

Tasek Lama is also a popular spot for hiking every week or even every day around the hills. For some, the lush green hills are ideal for scenic hikes, as you can see parts of the capital from the top of the hill. Some parts of the hill are cool because the area still retains most of the green.

With a total of seven hills, the list includes Helang Hills, Karamunting Hill, Laurel Hill, Markuching Hill, Luba Hill, Nangka and Sipinang Hills. All of these hills are also in line with Jalan Subok, ending at Lake Sarubing which is near the Subok Health Clinic. From end to end, the shortest route is about nine kilometers or more or five hours for beginners, although it is not recommended for beginners to use it. Among all the hills, Bukit Sarang Helang is the closest to the Tasek Lama with two main forest paths leading to the park area.

The 1.8-kilometre hike up to the peak of Bukit Sarang Helang has two rest stops with wooden benches where you can have a quick rest before continuing on your journey. I would recommend you to take your time and take in the jungle scenery, unless you’re in a rush or in a race of course. The most challenging part of the hike was to climb from the foot of Sarang Helang and the 128-metre-tall climbing trail was steep and was already straining.

Since then, the climb back to the Tasek Lama has been mostly declining, which is a bit tricky. To make it easier for hikers to find their way, signboards with maps has also been put in place along the forest track.
